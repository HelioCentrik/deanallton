About

Good data.
Better decisions.

I didn't plan to end up in BI. I made my way through accounts payable exceptions, moved into procurement QA, and eventually landed in data analytics. Each step followed the same instinct: something's off, let me figure out what. That's still how I approach most problems. Data work, at its best, feels like detective work.

Japan has intrigued me since I was a kid. Anime before it was cool, the food, the culture, the language I always wished I'd learned in school. It was never a career calculation. I'm studying Japanese now and working toward making the move.

On the work itself: I care about clean queries, well-structured models, and not overengineering things. I've inherited enough messy setups to know how much that stuff compounds. I try to leave things in a state I'd want to find them.
